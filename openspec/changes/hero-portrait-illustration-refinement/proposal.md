## Why

The current hero portrait lands too close to a studio-style photo illustration, which weakens the intended hand-drawn editorial identity of the portfolio. The portrait needs a clearer illustrative treatment and more domain-specific insignia so the hero feels more distinctive, intentional, and technically personal.

## What Changes

- Refine the existing hero portrait so it reads as semi-realistic digital illustration rather than photoreal portrait rendering.
- Push the image style toward editorial concept art, painterly monochrome shading, light inked treatment, and visibly hand-drawn stylization.
- Preserve the recognizable face, hoodie or sweatshirt silhouette, military-inspired honors aesthetic, and gold embroidered badge treatment.
- Replace the current generic engineering emblems with stitched insignias inspired by Node.js, .NET, PHP, Python, Go, and Java.
- Update the hero asset brief and related hero presentation details as needed so the refined portrait remains consistent with the premium portfolio direction.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `hero-portrait-asset-refinement`: Defines the new artistic direction, emblem requirements, preservation constraints, and acceptance criteria for the hero portrait asset used by the homepage.

### Modified Capabilities
- None.

## Impact

- Affected files will likely include the hero portrait asset under `src/assets/`, the hero section in `src/components/sections/HeroSection.tsx`, and any supporting content or notes used to describe the merit badges.
- No backend, API, routing, or hosting changes are required.
- The change remains scoped to the hero identity asset and its immediate presentation rather than a broader site redesign.
