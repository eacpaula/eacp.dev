## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Sketch-inspired page background
The system MUST refine the global page background so it feels visually consistent with the hero portrait illustration. The background MUST evoke a dark graphite, charcoal, or concept-art atmosphere and MAY include subtle layered texture cues inspired by sketch paper, charcoal shading, ink wash, or rough illustration backdrops. The texture treatment MUST remain restrained enough to preserve readability, professionalism, and frontend implementation realism.

#### Scenario: Reviewer inspects the page background
- **WHEN** the homepage is reviewed behind the hero and subsequent sections
- **THEN** the background feels closer to the portrait’s sketch-style atmosphere without becoming noisy, messy, or distracting

### Requirement: Monochrome and gold palette refinement
The system MUST keep the visual palette almost entirely black, white, and gray, with gold as the primary accent color. Gold MAY be used for buttons, borders, labels, dividers, highlights, and emphasis details. The system MUST avoid colorful UI accents and MUST avoid gradients that feel soft, startup-like, or disconnected from the illustrated hero.

#### Scenario: Stakeholder reviews the site palette
- **WHEN** the refined site surfaces and controls are compared to the hero illustration
- **THEN** the palette reads as premium, monochromatic, and editorial with restrained gold emphasis rather than multicolor interface styling

### Requirement: Sketch-aligned surface and card language
The system MUST refine cards, panels, section shells, and content containers so they feel integrated with the sketch-inspired visual language of the portrait. Surfaces MUST favor subtle border work, layered depth, low-contrast panel separation, and restrained texture cues over flat generic dashboard panels or sterile dark boxes.

#### Scenario: User scans representative content sections
- **WHEN** a reviewer moves from the hero into summary, impact, skills, timeline, case studies, writing, and contact sections
- **THEN** the cards and panels feel like part of the same charcoal-and-ink visual system as the portrait

### Requirement: Hero environment consistency
The system MUST make the hero section feel visually unified with the illustrated portrait. The left portrait panel, right content area, and global background MUST feel like they belong to the same world, and the page background MUST support the portrait rather than compete with it.

#### Scenario: Reviewer evaluates the hero as one composition
- **WHEN** the hero section is reviewed as a whole
- **THEN** the portrait frame, text area, and surrounding atmosphere read as one cohesive visual environment

### Requirement: Whole-site reusability
The system MUST define the refined visual system so it scales consistently across the rest of the portfolio, including summary, impact, skills, timeline, case studies, writing, and contact sections. The rules for texture, borders, depth, and emphasis MUST be reusable instead of depending on one-off hero-only styling.

#### Scenario: Design system is applied outside the hero
- **WHEN** the refined style is extended to other homepage sections
- **THEN** those sections can reuse the same background logic, palette rules, surface language, and accent behavior without introducing conflicting visual patterns

### Requirement: Texture restraint and UX quality
The system MUST maintain strong contrast, accessibility, and recruiter-friendly clarity while introducing the sketch-inspired mood. The site MUST remain minimalist, intentional, and readable. The system MUST NOT overuse texture, MUST NOT become cluttered, MUST NOT feel like a game interface or fantasy artwork, and MUST remain realistic to implement in a modern frontend codebase.

#### Scenario: Accessibility and professionalism review
- **WHEN** the refined style is evaluated for usability and tone
- **THEN** content remains readable, contrast remains strong, and the design still feels professional rather than theatrical or noisy
